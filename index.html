🏆 Band 7+ Model Answer

The bar chart illustrates the proportions of Australian men and women who engaged in five distinct leisure activities in 2023.

Overall, it is clear that watching TV was the most popular pastime for both genders, while reading and playing sports showed the most significant gender differences. Generally, men preferred more active or screen-based leisure, whereas women showed higher participation in reading.

Looking at the most popular activities, watching TV was highly favoured by both men (85%) and women (80%). Socialising was also common, with slightly more women (75%) participating compared to men (70%).

However, distinct gender preferences emerged in the remaining categories. Playing sports was considerably more popular among men (60%) than women (35%). Conversely, reading was much more prevalent among women (65%) compared to men (40%). Finally, gardening attracted a similar, moderate percentage of both men (45%) and women (50%).

💡 Tips for Band 7

  • Include a clear Overview paragraph starting with "Overall,..."
  • Group the data — don't list every number one by one
  • Use comparisons: "whereas", "conversely", "significantly higher"
  • Use varied vocabulary: "proportion", "percentage", "participation rate"
  • Write at least 150 words (aim for 170–190)
Task 2 — Essay
⏱ 40:00 🎯 Min 250 words
Question: Some people think that technology has made our lives more complex, while others believe it has made them simpler.

Discuss both these views and give your own opinion.
Words: 0 / 250 minimum

🏆 Band 7+ Model Answer

The impact of technology on daily life is a subject of frequent debate. While some argue that technological advancements have complicated our existence, others contend that they have streamlined our routines. This essay will examine both perspectives before concluding that technology ultimately simplifies life.

On the one hand, those who argue that technology makes life more complex point to the steep learning curves and constant updates. For instance, older generations often struggle to adapt to digital banking, feeling alienated by systems that were once simple. Furthermore, digital communication tools, such as emails, have blurred the lines between work and personal life. Employees are now expected to be reachable 24/7, leading to increased stress and a more complicated work-life balance.

On the other hand, the argument that technology simplifies life is highly compelling. The primary reason is the unprecedented access to information and services. Tasks that previously took hours, such as researching for an essay or booking a flight, can now be completed in minutes. Additionally, automation handles mundane household chores. For example, robotic vacuum cleaners manage home maintenance with minimal human intervention, making daily living significantly easier.

In my opinion, while technology introduces new challenges, such as digital fatigue, the benefits far outweigh the drawbacks. The core function of technology is to solve problems, and it has undeniably made fundamental aspects of life much more efficient.

In conclusion, although technology can complicate life by demanding constant adaptation, its ability to automate tasks and provide instant access to information makes it a profoundly simplifying force.

💡 Tips for Band 7

  • Use a 4-paragraph structure: Intro, View 1, View 2 + Opinion, Conclusion
  • Use complex sentences: "Although...", "While some argue..., others..."
  • Include specific examples to support each point
  • Make your opinion clear throughout, not just at the end
  • Write at least 250 words (aim for 270–290)
